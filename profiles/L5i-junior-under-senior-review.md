# Profile L5i: Junior practitioner under senior review

**Status: PRELIMINARY — skeleton (full content TBD)**

**Lifecycle stage**: L5 (practitioner-user) + L6 (multi-user collaborator)
**Shape variation**: practitioner-shape
**Archetype**: any (junior in any practitioner-shape archetype)
**Scale**: small to large firm (any scale supporting hierarchical authority)

## Identity + context

Junior practitioner (associate, junior consultant, postdoc, resident, junior partner) working under senior practitioner's authority. Drafts claims; senior reviews + signs; junior's name attached but senior bears ultimate accountability. Career-development arc: junior's expertise growing toward eventual senior judgment.

Hierarchical authority dynamics: junior produces; senior reviews; senior signs. Per-claim authority chain: junior-drafted-claim + senior-attestation = defensible authorship.

Adds **hierarchical collaboration + explicit capacity-building** dimension. PBS's capacity-building value claim is concrete here — junior's expertise grows through engaged review chains.

## What this profile is intended to EXEMPLIFY

This profile stress-tests:
- **Hierarchical authority binding**: per-claim, junior-drafts + senior-attests; authority chain captured in audit-trail
- **Per-claim review semantics**: which claims senior reviews vs trusts junior; review-granularity policy per shape
- **Capacity-building manifestation**: junior's pattern-recognition develops through engagement; ad-hoc → codified at junior's emerging scope
- **Authorship attribution**: junior drafts; senior signs; both names on output; per-claim attribution shows actual claim-author
- **Mentor-mentee feedback**: senior's review feedback is capacity-building input; framework should support this
- **Trust-graduating dynamics**: senior trusts junior more over time; review intensity decreases for proven areas
- **Senior-becomes-junior elsewhere**: senior practitioner is junior in adjacent area (e.g., senior litigator is junior in IP); authority is per-domain not per-person
- **Cross-firm career mobility**: junior moves to new firm; capacity-building captured (or not?) by framework follows them
- **Junior's defensibility under challenge**: if junior eventually defends own claim post-departure, can they reconstruct senior-reviewed reasoning?
