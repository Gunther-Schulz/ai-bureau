# Profile L8: Auditor / reviewer — post-hoc defensibility test

**Status: PRELIMINARY — skeleton (full content TBD)**

**Lifecycle stage**: L8 (evaluator stage; post-hoc framework engagement)
**Shape variation**: any (evaluates outputs from any shape)
**Archetype**: regulator / peer-reviewer / opposing-counsel / professional-board / external-auditor
**Scale**: typically external entity (not embedded in practitioner's workspace)

## Identity + context

Person or entity engaging with framework outputs **post-hoc** — months or years after work was produced — applying the defensibility test under regulatory, professional, or adversarial scrutiny. Examples:

- Regulator examining auditor's audit working papers (PCAOB inspection; SEC enforcement)
- Peer reviewer reviewing manuscript (academic peer review; double-blind review)
- Opposing counsel cross-examining lawyer's brief reasoning (litigation cross-examination)
- Professional-board reviewing medical case notes (medical-board investigation)
- UNB / TÖB reviewing planner's Begründung argumentation (administrative review)
- Bar discipline reviewing lawyer's filings (bar grievance investigation)

Distinct from L5d (auditor as practitioner) — L8 is the EXTERNAL evaluator engaging WITH framework outputs, not the practitioner producing within framework. L8 is consumer of the output's defensibility evidence.

## What this profile is intended to EXEMPLIFY

This profile stress-tests:
- **Defensibility test scenario from evaluator side**: what does the regulator / reviewer actually need to challenge? What evidence?
- **Reasoning chain reconstructability**: L8 traces practitioner's reasoning chain via audit-trail; per-claim source-grounding; per-claim sparring outcomes
- **Six-months-later test reality**: L8 engagement may be 6 months, 2 years, 5 years post-production
- **Audit-trail integrity**: workspace audit-trail must survive intact across deployments / migrations
- **Engaged-authorship verification**: L8 distinguishes engaged-authorship from rubber-stamping; how?
- **Per-claim defensibility resolution**: L8 challenges specific claims; framework's per-claim audit must support per-claim challenge
- **Cross-deployment evidence**: practitioner moved firms / migrated workspace; L8 evidence must follow
- **External-format requirements**: L8 may not access framework directly; needs exportable audit-trail in standard formats (PDF reports; CSV event logs; etc.)
- **Adversarial vs neutral evaluation**: opposing counsel adversarial; peer reviewer neutral; professional board investigative — different evidence requirements
