# Profile L5c: Researcher in lab

**Status: PRELIMINARY — skeleton (full content TBD)**

**Lifecycle stage**: L5 (practitioner-user)
**Shape variation**: practitioner-shape
**Archetype**: researcher (academic or industry research)
**Scale**: small lab (PI + 3-7 grad students / postdocs / staff researchers)

## Identity + context

Principal investigator + lab members in academic or industry research lab. Domain varies (life sciences, social sciences, engineering, computer science). Produces accountability-bearing research outputs (manuscripts, grant proposals, datasets, code, presentations) under peer-review scrutiny + funder-accountability + institutional ethics review (IRB, IACUC) + publication-ethics standards.

Multi-user case: PI + lab members work on shared manuscripts; co-authorship hierarchies; review chains; mentor-mentee relationships. Multi-practitioner workspace with hierarchical authority.

Adds variation to L5a planner / L5b lawyer — collaborative authorship scenario; different regulatory shape (peer review + ethics + funder vs administrative law / court rules); manuscript-as-work-unit with multiple contributors.

## What this profile is intended to EXEMPLIFY

This profile stress-tests:
- **Multi-practitioner workspace** with hierarchical authority (PI + grad students + postdocs)
- **Co-authorship workflow_instance ownership**: who owns workflow_instance for shared manuscript? primary_practitioner = first-author? PI? rotates by phase?
- **Multi-user authority binding**: PI signs final claims; grad students draft; per-claim attribution to actual claim-author
- **Capacity-building emphasis**: junior lab members learn through engagement; framework's capacity-building value claim concrete here
- **Funder accountability**: grant deliverables tracked; reproducibility requirements; data-management plans
- **Research-ethics defensibility**: IRB/IACUC compliance; ethics review post-hoc challenges
- **Reproducibility + data-grounding**: per-claim source-grounding for empirical claims; methodology defensibility under peer review
- **Multi-output work-unit** (manuscript + dataset + code + supplement; one work-unit produces multiple deliverables)
- **Long-arc work**: multi-year research projects; workflow_instances span many sessions over months
- **Cross-lab collaboration**: external co-authors; multi-institutional papers
