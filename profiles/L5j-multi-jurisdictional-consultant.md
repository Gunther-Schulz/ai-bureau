**Status: PRELIMINARY — skeleton (full content TBD)**

**Archetype**: cross-jurisdictional consultant (tax, immigration, cross-border M&A, etc.)
**Scale**: solo or small specialty practice

## Identity + context

Practitioner specializing in cross-jurisdictional work: tax advisor handling DE + AT + CH clients; immigration lawyer with US + EU clients; M&A consultant on transactions spanning 3+ jurisdictions. Each client/project may invoke 2-N jurisdictional regulatory frameworks simultaneously.

Adds **shape composition + cross-jurisdiction** stress test. Single-jurisdiction profiles miss the architectural challenges of overlapping-but-distinct regulatory environments.

## What this profile is intended to EXEMPLIFY

This profile stress-tests:
- **Multi-jurisdictional Layer A** activation: workspace's `scope.states` includes multiple jurisdictions simultaneously
- **Cross-jurisdictional shape composition**: practitioner-shape applies; jurisdiction-specific shape variants compose (DE-tax-shape + AT-tax-shape + CH-tax-shape)
- **Per-jurisdiction defensibility**: same claim may need to defend under different jurisdictions' standards; multiple regulatory authorities
- **Conflicting regulatory requirements**: when DE law conflicts with AT law for same transaction; resolution mechanics
- **Per-jurisdiction specialist activation**: jurisdiction-specific specialists (DE-tax-specialist + AT-tax-specialist + CH-tax-specialist) all active simultaneously
- **Layer A scope dynamics**: per-project Layer A may activate different jurisdictional content; per-client variation
- **Cross-language content** (DE + EN + FR for CH; etc.): Layer A multi-language requirements
- **Per-jurisdiction audit-emission requirements**: different jurisdictions may have different audit-retention / privacy / disclosure requirements
- **Specialist-as-jurisdictional-expertise**: shape catalog curator (L9) maintains per-jurisdiction shape variants; consultant uses curated set
- **Citizenship + residency complexities**: client jurisdictions vs practitioner jurisdiction; differing applicability
