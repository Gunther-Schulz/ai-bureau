## Identity + context

Per VISION's mention of autonomous-business-shape as an alternate framework shape: a business that runs itself with AI as load-bearing operational mechanism. Different value claims than practitioner-shape:
- Less emphasis on practitioner-author defensibility (no individual practitioner signing every output)
- More emphasis on autonomous decision-making within business policy
- Different audit + governance + accountability shape
- AI-as-runtime more central; less HITL per-claim

Hypothetical-but-realistic: an autonomous accounting service for small businesses; an autonomous customer-support business; an autonomous research-aggregation service.

This profile is the most-divergent from practitioner-shape and stress-tests framework's claim to be shape-neutral at primitive level (with shape policies configuring the differences).

## What this profile is intended to EXEMPLIFY

This profile stress-tests:
- **Shape-neutrality of framework primitives** — workspace, specialist, claim, etc. work for autonomous-business as well as practitioner-shape
- **Per-shape policy variation**: autonomous-business-shape's policies differ substantially from practitioner-shape (audit granularity; HITL requirements; sparring frequency; claim-attestation)
- **AI-as-runtime more central**: less HITL; more autonomous decision-making within policy boundaries
- **Different defensibility shape**: business-level accountability vs individual-practitioner-level
- **Capacity-building absent**: autonomous-business doesn't have a "practitioner growing through engagement" axis — what does axis-2 sparring mean here?
- **Different category-collapse manifestations**: rubber-stamping (axis-3) doesn't apply directly when there's no individual practitioner-author; what's the autonomous-business analog?
- **Scaling concerns**: autonomous-business may have many concurrent decisions; multi-instance scaling
- **Per-customer interaction patterns**: business serves many customers; each interaction may produce work-unit
