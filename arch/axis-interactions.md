## 2. Per-axis structural overview

### 2.1 Axis 1 — intertwining (workflow embedding)

**Anchor**: `glossary/intertwining.md` canonical body + `VISION.md` §The thesis (axis 1 contrast tables) + §Implications "Workflow as precondition (axis 1)".

**Architectural articulation**: axis-1 success mode = `intertwined-ai` per `glossary/intertwined-ai.md` (AI participates at workflow handoff points without explicit invocation; AI output composes into work artifacts; AI tracks work state across sessions; sparring fires WITHIN work-production not as separate review step); failure mode = `tacked-on-ai` per `glossary/tacked-on-ai.md` (AI as discrete convenience-feature bolted onto unchanged human workflow; no cross-session state; no orchestration awareness; no colleague-grade discipline). The relational frame characterizing axis-1 success is `co-worker` per `glossary/co-worker.md` (the categorical role-claim that AI runtime participates in workflow as colleague in actual production of accountability-bearing work — distinct from "AI as tool" / "AI as assistant" / "AI as oracle").

**Architectural mechanisms enabling axis-1**: substrate Surface §B hooks + §F session/context (per `arch/substrate.md` realize cross-session axis-1 mechanisms); workflow primitive (per `arch/workflow-work-unit.md` — workflow IS what intertwined AI intertwines WITH per `glossary/workflow.md` axis-1 PRIMARY anchor); authority-binding mechanism (per `glossary/authority-binding.md` — axis-1 trust requires no anonymous-actor / silent-AI-action; intertwined AI participates with attributed-emission). Workflow as precondition per VISION line 199: domains with rich, structured workflows (planning / law / engineering / healthcare / accounting) are natural fits — there's a real pattern of work to embed in.

### 2.2 Axis 2 — sparring (interaction mode)

**Anchor**: `glossary/sparring.md` canonical body + `VISION.md` §Sparring partner, not answer machine + §Foundations Vivienne Ming.

**Architectural articulation**: axis-2 grounded in Vivienne Ming's 4-mode research per VISION line 100 — only the third mode (sparring) produces value; the other three modes are independent failure shapes per locked GLOSSARY: `oracle-ai` (declarative-direction failure; humans submit AI's answer as their own; performance same as AI alone), `validator-ai` (affirmation-direction failure; humans ask AI to support preconceptions; sycophancy loop; performance worse than AI alone), `answer-machine-ai` (extraction-direction failure; humans extract output without sparring resistance). All three failure modes are independent dimensions per `glossary/answer-machine-ai.md` line 27 ("Not mutually exclusive with oracle / validator").

**Architectural mechanisms enabling axis-2**: per `arch/sparring.md` §4, the Sparring mechanism class collects 8 sub-mechanism categories under per-shape policy, partitioned into:
- 4 architecturally-encoded gate-dispatched sub-mechanisms (counter-argument as first-class output / confidence calibration / visible reasoning / selective friction) leveraging substrate Surface §D structured output validation
- 4 behaviorally-enforced sub-mechanisms (anti-sycophancy / asymmetric knowledge respect / commit-to-recommendations / what's-missing) applied at AI judgment time per prose-convention skill body

Sparring fires AT claim granularity per `glossary/sparring.md` (counter-arguments target individual claims; confidence calibration applies per claim; selective friction triggers per claim ambiguity).

### 2.3 Axis 3 — authorship preservation (outcome orientation)

**Anchor**: `glossary/authorship-preservation.md` canonical body + `VISION.md` §Authorship preservation, not rubber-stamping + §The defensibility test (six-months-later test).

**Architectural articulation**: axis-3 success criterion is the **defensibility test** per VISION line 168: "Will the practitioner-author be able to defend this output six months from now under regulatory or professional challenge, having forgotten the details?" The operational success mode is `engaged-authorship` per `glossary/engaged-authorship.md` 2-phase composite per-claim test:
1. **Production-phase engagement** (axis-2-anchored): per-claim sparring participation observed via sparring-event emissions DURING claim production. Without production-phase engagement, claim production was answer-machine / oracle / validator extraction (axis-2 failure modes); claim's engaged-authorship condition fails at production phase.
2. **Attestation-phase engagement** (axis-3-anchored): per-claim attestation event emitted at finalization (NOT whole-output sign-off). Without per-claim attestation, claim was rubber-stamped (axis-3 failure mode); claim's engaged-authorship condition fails at attestation phase.

Both phases are independent and both must structurally complete. Failure mode is `rubber-stamping` per `glossary/rubber-stamping.md` (practitioner-author signs off on AI-produced content without engaging the reasoning, judgment calls, or claim-by-claim defensibility; HITL ceremony complete; cognitive engagement absent).

**Architectural mechanisms enabling axis-3**: per `arch/claim-defensibility.md` §3 per-claim attestation chain mechanics (6-step ordered emissions: `claim_made` → production-phase sparring events → revision events → finalization → per-claim attestation event → composition INTO work-unit attribution chain); audit-trail-as-canonical-source per `arch/audit.md` §10 (audit storage available BEFORE substrate emits first architectural event AND audit storage shuts down AFTER substrate releases — boot-before-substrate / shutdown-after-substrate ordering); authority-binding mechanism per `glossary/authority-binding.md` (per-event actor declaration + per-claim author attribution + authority-decision binding — reconstructible attribution chain is precondition for defensibility's reconstructible-reasoning-chain condition Cond #2).

## 3. Cross-axis composition WITHIN cluster

### 3.1 Pairwise composition relationships

**(2 within 1) Sparring fires WITHIN intertwined co-work** — axis-2 requires axis-1 as substrate. Per VISION line 43: "Tacked-on but well-designed sparring (a 'find weaknesses' button) is still tacked-on (axis 1 failure)." Sparring as load-bearing runtime mechanism cannot be hosted by tacked-on AI — the relational frame `co-worker` per `glossary/co-worker.md` is the precondition for genuine sparring per `glossary/intertwined-ai.md` line 22 ("Sparring (axis 2) fires within work-production, not as a separate 'review' step (axis-2 activates within axis-1 intertwining; per VISION line 43 axis-interaction)").

**(3 evaluates 1+2 outputs) Defensibility resolves AT axis-1 work-products** — axis-3 tests claims that emerged through axes 1+2. Per `arch/scope-model.md` §4 E3 content-unit-IN-instance pattern: claim is content-unit IN work-unit instance (INHERITS work-unit's Owner B placement). Per `arch/claim-defensibility.md` §3: per-claim defensibility composes work-unit defensibility — if every claim passes the test, the work-unit's output passes; ONE indefensible claim taints the work-unit's output. Defensibility is a property/test APPLIED TO claims (not entity-having); the test resolves at claim granularity through per-claim attribution chain composing across Framework C definitions (skill identifier → specialist DEFINITION) + Owner B instances (workspace + work-unit instance + practitioner-RECORD).

**(2 → 3 production substrate) Sparring events ARE production-phase substrate for engaged-authorship Cond #1** — axis-2 → axis-3 dependency. Per `glossary/sparring.md` composes-with engaged-authorship row + `glossary/engaged-authorship.md` line 36 ("production-phase engagement is sparring-anchored; sparring events are the production-phase signal substrate"). Per `arch/claim-defensibility.md` §3 attestation chain step 2: "Production-phase engaged-authorship sparring events (axis-2 substrate) — sparring sub-mechanisms accessed by skills DURING claim production per `arch/sparring.md` §4." Without production-phase sparring events, engaged-authorship Cond #1 fails at production phase regardless of attestation-phase event presence.

### 3.2 Triple integration (1+2+3)

Per VISION line 43 (the formula): "PBS aims for all three: **intertwined sparring partnership in service of defensible authorship.**" Per `VISION.md` §Trust + sparring + authorship together: three forms of protection composing into one architecture (trust mechanisms protecting practitioner FROM the AI; sparring mechanisms protecting practitioner FROM comfortable but degrading interaction patterns; authorship mechanisms protecting practitioner's professional/legal standing). The triple integration is what distinguishes PBS from architectures satisfying any 1 or 2 axes alone.

### 3.3 Asymmetric composition (load-bearing)

Per VISION lines 39-43: "A system can fail on any axis independently." But composition is NOT symmetric — directional dependency:
- **axis-1 FOUNDATIONAL**: substrate for axes 2+3. Tacked-on AI cannot host genuine sparring (axis-2 failure cascades from axis-1 failure); tacked-on AI's outputs lack the reasoning chain reconstructable axis-3 requires (axis-3 failure cascades from axis-1 failure)
- **axis-2 OPERATIONAL within axis-1**: sparring fires AT claim granularity DURING work-production within intertwined co-work; sparring events ARE production-phase substrate for axis-3 engaged-authorship Cond #1
- **axis-3 EVALUATIVE about 1+2 OUTPUTS**: defensibility test resolves AT claim granularity AGAINST work-products produced through axes 1+2; axis-3 attestation-phase failure (rubber-stamping) is INDEPENDENTLY possible at finalization moment regardless of axes 1+2 success at production phase

The directional dependency is captured per VISION line 41: "Tacked-on but well-designed sparring (a 'find weaknesses' button) is still tacked-on (axis 1 failure)." Reverse direction does NOT hold: intertwined AI without sparring is axis-2 failure, NOT axis-1 failure (axis-1 succeeds even when axis-2 fails); intertwined sparring without per-claim attestation is axis-3 failure, NOT axes 1+2 failure (axes 1+2 succeed even when axis-3 fails at attestation).

### 3.4 CC-1 — Cross-axis failure cascade pattern

Three independent failure modes; one directional cascade. **Load-bearing structural commitment** (per `VISION.md` lines 39-43 axis-failure framing made explicit as cross-axis structural commitment):

- **axis-1 failure (tacked-on) → forecloses axes 2+3**: tacked-on AI cannot host genuine sparring (axis-2 forecloses) AND cannot produce reasoning chains the practitioner can reconstruct under regulatory challenge (axis-3 forecloses). One-way cascade: axis-1 failure CASCADES TO axes 2+3.
- **axis-2 failure (oracle / validator / answer-machine) → cascades to axis-3 production-phase**: per `glossary/answer-machine-ai.md` + `glossary/oracle-ai.md` + `glossary/validator-ai.md` composes-with engaged-authorship row — production-phase sparring engagement is bypassed under axis-2 failure mode; engaged-authorship Cond #1 fails at production phase; defensibility test fails per claim. One-way cascade: axis-2 failure CASCADES TO axis-3 production-phase.
- **axis-3 failure (rubber-stamping at attestation) is INDEPENDENTLY possible**: per `glossary/rubber-stamping.md` line 27 ("Not mutually exclusive with axis-1 / axis-2 failures — same workspace can have tacked-on AI + answer-machine AI + rubber-stamping co-occurring; these are independent failure dimensions"). axis-3 attestation-phase failure can occur EVEN WITH axes 1+2 success at production phase.

`category-collapse` (cross-axis force per `glossary/category-collapse.md`) is the general force pattern producing per-axis manifestations; CC-1 is the directional cascade structure those manifestations follow. The cascade pattern is load-bearing for cross-axis observability (per E2): per-axis signals must be detected at the axis where the failure FIRES, not downstream where it cascades.

### 3.6 E6 — Per-claim cross-axis lifecycle composition

Per `arch/claim-defensibility.md` §3 per-claim attestation chain mechanics + `glossary/engaged-authorship.md` per-claim per-version semantics, per-claim cross-axis lifecycle composes ordered events spanning axes 2+3:
