### Round 2 — 8 sharpening refinements (RATIONALE)

**R1 Counter-VISION engagement (EXPANSION)** — added subsection within "The thesis"

Engaged with strongest opposing thesis (Sequoia/Bek service-as-software). PBS rejects specifically for high-stakes regulated work where accountability cannot be delegated. Sparring + authorship-preservation = alternative to delegation. Cited Stanford "workslop" research as empirical critique. EU AI Act + Berufsrecht structurally rewards practitioner-as-deployer posture. Cherry Ventures thesis match named.

**R2 Negative space articulation (EXPANSION)** — added "What VISION does NOT claim" subsection

Drew boundary explicitly: PBS does NOT claim practitioners obsolete; does NOT replace human judgment; does NOT optimize speed-over-capacity; does NOT treat sparring as universally right interaction mode (oracle is right for fact lookup); does NOT make humans unnecessary; AI-as-co-worker doesn't mean AI does bulk; framework is not restricted to practitioner shape (per layered approach).

**R3 Temporal robustness (EXPANSION)** — added "Robustness to AI capability growth" subsection

All three axes are accuracy-independent. Better AI strengthens (not weakens) the thesis: capacity-preservation matters more as AI accuracy increases (temptation to skip exploration grows); accountability cannot be delegated regardless of AI capability; regulatory frameworks tighten with better AI.

**R4 VISION ≠ ARCHITECTURE explicit boundary (EXPANSION)** — added new section near document end

Crisp delineation: VISION = WHY (value claims); ARCHITECTURE = HOW (structural primitives); disciplines = HOW serving WHY. Concrete mapping table for 6 axis-implementation pairs. Decision-rule for placement: value claim → VISION; structural primitive → ARCHITECTURE; positioning choice → strategic-positioning.md.

**R5 Architectural inheritance / Option B structural floor (REVISION — load-bearing)** — surfaced in Round 2 + locked session 14

Resolves real tension: framework is workspace-shape-neutral; can other shapes built on framework violate axis 3?

**Option A** (framework hosts visions PBS doesn't endorse) vs **Option B** (framework structurally enforces axis 3 floor regardless of shape).

Locked **Option B** per Make-wrong-shapes-impossible discipline + EU AI Act protection + PBS brand integrity + practitioner protection. Specifically: anti-Art-25-trap gate (specialist conformity manifest) + claim-level audit emission + human authority somewhere in accountability chain are STRUCTURAL. Cannot be disabled without explicit framework override (produces non-PBS-conformant deployment).

Other shapes can configure axis intensities (sparring optional vs always-on; audit granularity at action-level vs claim-level) but cannot disable structural accountability binding.

**Sharpening question resolved**: yes, autonomous-business shape (Paperclip-style) still works under Option B — human authority granularity is configurable (per-output / per-decision-class / per-policy / per-budget-cycle / per-specialist-installation). What's blocked: zero-human accountability-bearing deployments.

Real-world parallel: autonomous trading systems (algos execute thousands of trades autonomously, but human risk officer authorized strategy + position limits + audit trail captures every decision + compliance gate prevents algo from changing strategy autonomously).
